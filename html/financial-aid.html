Financial Aid Guides

These guides turn complex scheme information into simple language for single parents in Singapore. For full details, always check the official government websites linked in each card.

Not sure where to start?

Answer a few quick questions and we will suggest a scheme that may be a good starting point. This is not an official assessment.

Key schemes for single parents

Below are simplified explanations. Tap “View official site” on each card to confirm the latest information.

ComCare Short-to-Medium Term Assistance

Daily living support

Monthly cash and other support to help with basic living expenses for low-income households.

Who it may help

  • Single parents who are unemployed or earning very low income.
  • Household has little or no savings and faces difficulty paying for daily needs.
  • Singapore Citizens or Permanent Residents living in Singapore.

What support can look like

  • Monthly cash for food and daily expenses.
  • Help with Service & Conservancy Charges and utilities bills.
  • Assistance with rental for HDB flats.

Simple application checklist

  1. Prepare NRICs of all household members.
  2. Collect recent payslips or income documents (if any).
  3. Gather bank statements and HDB rental/loan documents.
  4. Visit your nearest Social Service Office (SSO) or apply online via the official site.

KidSTART

Early childhood support

Support for lower-income families to give young children a strong start in health, learning, and development.

Who it may help

  • Pregnant mothers and families with children aged 0–6 years old.
  • Lower-income households, usually staying in rental flats or with lower per capita income.
  • Families who may need extra support with parenting and child development.

What support can look like

  • Home visits by professionals to support parenting and child development.
  • Priority access to quality preschools under KidSTART.
  • Support for health, nutrition, and social-emotional development.

Simple application checklist

  1. Speak to your hospital social worker or Family Service Centre social worker.
  2. Prepare NRIC and birth certificates for your children.
  3. Share more about your family situation with the officer or social worker.
  4. Wait for follow-up from the KidSTART team on your eligibility.

Higher Education Community Bursary

Education bursary

Financial help for students from lower- and middle-income households in JC, Millennia Institute, Polytechnics and ITE.

Who it may help

  • Singapore Citizen students in JC, MI, Polytechnics, or ITE.
  • Households with lower or middle income (based on gross household income or per capita income).
  • Single-parent families who need help paying for school fees and daily expenses.

What support can look like

  • Subsidies for tuition fees.
  • Yearly bursary for living expenses, textbooks, and transport.

Simple application checklist

  1. Check with your child’s school or institution’s website for bursary details and forms.
  2. Prepare NRICs, student card, and proof of household income.
  3. Submit the bursary application online or through the school’s student portal.
  4. Attend any interview if requested by the school.
